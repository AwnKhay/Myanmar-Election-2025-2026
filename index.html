Myanmar General Election 2025–2026

Phased polls held December 2025 – January 2026 under military administration

Overview

The 2025–2026 Myanmar general election was conducted in three phases (28 Dec 2025, 11 Jan 2026, 25 Jan 2026) by the military junta following the 2021 coup. Voting occurred only in selected townships due to ongoing civil conflict. Many opposition parties were banned or dissolved, and the process was widely described by the UN, Western governments, and human rights groups as a "sham" election aimed at legitimizing military rule rather than reflecting genuine democratic will.

The military-backed Union Solidarity and Development Party (USDP) won an overwhelming majority in both houses of parliament, virtually guaranteeing continued control by Senior General Min Aung Hlaing and allies.

Key Reported Facts

  • Phases: Dec 28, 2025 → Jan 11, 2026 → Jan 25, 2026
  • Winner: USDP (military proxy) – landslide victory
  • Turnout: Around 50–55% in contested areas (much lower than 2020)
  • Context: Held amid civil war; large areas had no voting; thousands of political prisoners; heavy repression
  • International view: Criticized as illegitimate by UN, EU, US, Human Rights Watch, etc.
